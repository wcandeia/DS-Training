Histograma: frequência no eixo y e variável eixo x. Ex.: frequência (y) vs idade (x)
Variável em x é numérica e quantitativa, diferente do gráfico de barras onde a variável
é categórica e qualitativa.

Frequency is on the y-axis; countries and colors are categorical data. With histograms, the x-axis should be numerical.
With frequency tables, we have exact counts, so we can always create the histogram. But not the opposite way around.
Frequency table: is better for calculating n.
Histograma: good for visualizing the shape of a distribution; can always be created if you have the other (table/histogram).
Frequency table: good for calculating the total number in the sample/population.
With tables, you can add the frequencies in each bin. With a histogram, you don't always know the exact frequencies.
A histogram lets you visually see the shape of a distribution (of data).
